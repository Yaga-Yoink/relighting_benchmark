2D Relighting Benchmarks of Transparent and Specular Objects

Benchmarking Neural Gaffer on Transparent Objects

Abstract

We evaluate Neural Gaffer, a neural relighting method, on transparent and specular objects—a setting largely missing from prior benchmarks. Transparent materials introduce strong light transport phenomena such as refraction and caustics, which challenge conventional relighting models. To address this, we introduce a custom dataset of transparent objects captured under varied illumination and provide quantitative and qualitative benchmarks before and after finetuning.

Method Overview

Neural Gaffer learns environment-conditioned neural relighting operators. While effective on opaque objects, performance degrades on transparent materials. We extend Neural Gaffer via finetuning on our transparent dataset, enabling the model to capture refractive and reflective light interactions.
